About me.

Who is Jeremy Soh?

Jeremy is a creative technologist, professional bug squasher, devoted animal lover, and always eager for a road trip to discover the hidden gems of the world.

Personally

I first stumbled into coding through my personal blog, where I experimented with customizing HTML templates just for fun. That small step unlocked an ongoing fascination with coding within me. Soon, I was exploring languages like JavaScript, Python, and more, and eventually diving into IT studies cemented my love for software development.

Alongside this journey, I completed a Diploma in Business IT from Ngee Ann Polytechnic, followed by a Bachelor of IT (double major) in Mobile and Web Application Development, and Web Communication (Digital Marketing), from Murdoch University via Kaplan. Since my blogging days, I have also casually explored digital arts, which has enhanced my ability to create user-friendly and visually appealing products.

As an introvert, I bring a thoughtful and reflective approach to my work, which not only fuels my passion for coding but also enables me to engage meaningfully in collaborative settings.

Outside of coding, I stay busy with weekly runs, being a caring plant dad to my vibrant succulents, spreading joy by sharing funny memes, and binge-watching everything from dramas to documentaries. I also love traveling and immersing myself in different cultures, with the hope of visiting every part of the world some day.

Professionally

There is nothing quite like the thrill of seeing my code come to life. I thrive on the challenges and problem-solving aspects of development, enjoying the process of creative solutions that meet real needs. Delivering a product that fully aligns with a client's vision is immensely rewarding for me.

I believe my diverse background helps me approach each project from unique angles. In my software development work, I prioritize user experience, functionality, clean code, and efficiency.

I am always eager to connect with others in the field, whether for collaboration, professional projects, or commission work. I also welcome any feedback you might have, as I value the insights and growth opportunities it brings.
